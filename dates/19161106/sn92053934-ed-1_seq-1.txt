The Argus, in conformity with time honored cus
tom, will print an extra issue Wednesday morn
ing givi?ig the returns of the election in the na
tion, state and county. The paper will be delivered
by carriers between 6 and 7 o'clock. Deliveries will
be made only to those who have ordered a copy in
advance. The carriers have canvassed subscribers
on their routes and these will be delivered copies at
five cents each. In previous years election extras
have been distributed among regular subscribers
without additional charge. The Argus, along with
other newspapers, finds it impossible this year to con
tinue that courtesy because of the increased cost in
the price of print paper. Hence the charge of five
cents per copy for the election extra. If you have not
arranged to have a copy of the election extra deliv
ered to you Wednesday morning you are advised to
telephone your order at once.
The Argus, as usual, will receive election returns
at its building Tuesday night. The figures from
the nation, state and county will be flashed on a
canvas opposite The Argus building. You are invit
ed to come and see how the election goes. The Ar
gus office will be open throughout the night to give
election information. If you can't come down to read
the bulletins you may use the telephone. The Argus
telephones are Nos. 145, 146 and 147.
