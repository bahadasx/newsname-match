BERLIN, Dec. 12. Germany and her allies today proposed to enter
forthwith into peace negotiations. The Austrian, Turkish and Bulgarian
governments are making similar proposals. These proposals also have been
transmitted to the Vatican.
The following announcement was given out today by the semi-official
Overseas News agency:
The chancellor this morning received one after another the representa
tives of the United States of America, Spain and Switzerland; that is, of the
states protecting German interests in hostile foreign countries. The chan
cellor transmitted to them a note and asked them to bring it to the attention
of the hostile governments. The note will be read in the reichstag today by
the chancellor. In the note the four allied (central) powers propose to enter
forthwith on peace negotiations. The propositions which they will bring for
such negotiations are, according to their firm belief, appropriate for the es
tablishment of a lasting peace. The governments at Vienna, Constanti
nople and Sofia transmitted identical notes, and also communicated with
the holy see and all neutral powers.
