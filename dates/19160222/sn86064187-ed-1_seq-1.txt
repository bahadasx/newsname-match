LEGISLATURE ADJOURNS SINE DIE;
FEW REMAIN TO WITNESS PASSING
Board of Affairs Takes Charge of Furnishings in Assembly
Chambers Extra Session Lasts Week Longer Than
Originally Intenclcct Closing Is Serene.
Thr extraordinary ncssion of tlir fifth Oklahoma legisla
ture is a thing of the past.
Both branches adjourned sine die at 11:45 o'clock Tues
day morning after the signing of a number of enrolled bills
by the presiding officers of the two branches in open session.
There was scarcely a quorum of either house present.
The journal record will show that adjournment was taken
at 9 o'clock, as that was the hour agreed upon Monday night.
The house clock was stopped at 9 o'clock and the senate clock
at 8:30 o'clock.
At noon the board of affairs took charge of the legislative
furniture and fixtures.
It will be just about ten and one-half months before the
regular session of the sixth legislature will convene.. That
session, it is expected, will be held in the new capitol.
The desks and chairs used in the regular and extra session
of the fifth legislature will be sold to members of the body.
I New furniture entirely will be used in the new capitol.
Closing came just a week late to make the session a "thirty
day" one as called by the governor. Peace marked the end of
a turbulent legislature. Compromises and amendments of con
tested bills at tho eleventh hour had settled, all quarrels ap
parently and the last morning was without features.
