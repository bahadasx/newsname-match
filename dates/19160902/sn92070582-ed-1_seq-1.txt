STRIKE DANGER OVER; SENATE PASSES
BILL, WHICH WILL BE RUSHED TO WILSON
WASHINGTON, Sept. 2.- The Adamson eight-hour bill
accepted by labor union officials as providing a satisfactory
settlement of the demands on which they base their order
calling a nation-wide railroad strike for Monday, was passed
without amendment by the senate tonight and now awaits
only President Wilson's signature to make it law. Revoca
tion of the strike order is expected within a few hours.
The vote on the bill was 43 to2S.
