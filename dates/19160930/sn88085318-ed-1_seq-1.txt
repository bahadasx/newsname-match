The fair at Hunters, which
closed a three day session last
Saturday was a pronounced suc
cess, and in many respects sur
passed all former fairs held in
Stevens county. The horticultural
display was the largest in variety
yet shown. The livestock exhibit
was the leading feature, cattle
ami hogs leading. A program of
sports, including athletics, races
ami Indian games, enlivened the
last day of the fair. It is estim
ated that 5000 attended, many
coming from Spokane, Davenpori
and upper Stevens county towns.
Music was furnished by the Che
welah and Hunters bands.
